**AI for Omics Research: Networking, Opportunities and Innovation**

On 23rd April 2026, we hosted an “AI x Omics” event, bringing together researchers from across UCL to discuss the opportunities and challenges presented by innovations in the respective technologies. 39 participants braved the tube strikes to attend and their enthusiasm made for a lively session. We didn’t end up using plans for structured discussion but felt this was more than compensated for by the conversations happening over coffee and lunch.

We had 10 excellent speakers from a range of disciplines. There was a large technical focus on incorporating agentic-LLMs to improve pipeline efficiency. A lot of speakers also discussed the projects integrating multimodal data, including different types of “omics” data as well as other modalities including imaging data and information on biological pathways. The applications were varied, going from fundamental cell biology, through biomedical diagnoses, and even wildlife and conservation ecology. The combination of innovation and research made for a fascinating session and we again thank the speakers for their contributions.

Feedback from the event was positive, with respondents rating it 4.9/5 for usefulness. The opportunity to see the breadth of research happening in this space was highlighted as a something people enjoyed. We also received some useful and instructive suggestions for next steps, including training, centrally managed tools, and for repeating this event in future! We know the event led to multiple conversations about new collaborations, too, which is a fantastic outcome. So thanks again to all participants and to AIBIO for the funding.
